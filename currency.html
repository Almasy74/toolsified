How to read the result

The page returns a live mid-market reference rate. That is useful for baseline comparisons, travel planning, invoice estimates, and quick FX sanity checks.

Limitations: banks, cards, and transfer services usually add their own spread and fees. The rate shown here is a reference estimate, not a guaranteed settlement quote.

Why does my bank show a different amount?

Providers apply spread, fees, and their own settlement timing, so their execution rate will differ from a market midpoint.

Should I use the hub or a pair page?

Use this hub for quick ad hoc checks across many currencies. Use a pair page when you want a familiar route, common amounts, and more pair-specific context.

What is the difference between mid-market and retail rates?

The mid-market rate is the midpoint between buy and sell prices on currency markets. Retail rates from banks and exchange services include a markup, so you will always receive less than the mid-market rate.

Can I use this for travel budgeting?

Yes, as a reference baseline. Keep in mind that ATM withdrawals, card transactions, and airport kiosks each add their own fees on top of the rate shown here.
