Method

How it works

The calculator applies a straightforward percentage formula.

  • gross = net x (1 + rate / 100) when the input is before tax
  • net = gross / (1 + rate / 100) when the input is after tax
  • tax amount = gross - net

Limitations: this is a planning calculator, not legal or accounting advice. Exemptions, mixed rates, import rules, reverse charge, and filing requirements are out of scope.

Is the preset list official tax guidance?

No. Presets are convenience shortcuts and should be verified against the actual rules that apply to your product, service, and jurisdiction.

Can I use this for US sales tax?

Yes, as a rough percentage calculator. It does not handle local filing rules, exemptions, or nexus.

What if I only know the tax-inclusive price?

Select "Price after tax" and the tool will back out the net amount and tax portion.

How does reverse VAT calculation work?

Divide the gross price by (1 + rate/100) to get the net amount, then subtract net from gross to find the tax portion.

Does VAT apply to cross-border purchases within the EU?

Rules vary by transaction type and threshold; B2C sales often use the destination country rate, while B2B may use reverse charge -- consult official guidance for your situation.

Why do VAT amounts sometimes differ by a penny due to rounding?

Tax authorities typically require rounding per line item, so small rounding differences can appear when you compare line-level and total-level calculations.
