How it works

The tool compounds annual inflation rates year by year from the selected start year to the selected end year.

Formula: adjusted value = amount x product(1 + annual rate).

Limitations: this is an educational estimate based on simplified annual CPI-like rates and does not replace official national statistics for legal or contractual use.

Why does compounding matter?

Inflation accumulates year over year, so long horizons can show large differences.

What is the difference between real and nominal value?

Nominal value is the amount in current dollars, while real value adjusts for inflation so you can compare purchasing power across different years.

How can I use this for salary negotiation?

Compare your current salary adjusted for cumulative inflation since your last raise to see whether your real pay has grown or shrunk.

What are the limitations of CPI as an inflation measure?

CPI tracks a fixed basket of goods that may not match your personal spending, and it can underweight fast-changing costs like housing or healthcare in your area.
