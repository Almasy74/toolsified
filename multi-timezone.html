Method

Why this works better than static offset math

The converter creates the reference instant in the chosen source zone and then renders that same moment in each comparison zone using official IANA time-zone definitions.

That matters because relative offsets change during the year when different regions enter and leave daylight saving on different dates.

Limitations: this is a time-alignment tool, not a scheduler. It does not check holidays, participant availability, or local work-hour policies.

Does this include daylight saving changes?

Yes. The selected date and time are evaluated with official time-zone rules for that moment.

Can I compare more than two locations?

Yes. Add as many comparison zones as you need and optionally show the difference matrix.

Why do offsets change during the year?

Regions switch clocks on different dates, and some do not switch at all, so the same pair can have different gaps across seasons.

What happens when zones span the International Date Line?

The converter handles date-line crossings automatically. The output will show the correct local date for each zone, even if it differs from the reference date.

How do I find a universal meeting window across many zones?

Add all relevant zones, compare the local times, and look for a slot that falls within working hours for every location. For automated overlap detection, try the Meeting Overlap Finder.

Can I save a set of timezone presets?

Not currently. Bookmark the page after adding your zones, or note the IANA names so you can re-add them quickly next time.
